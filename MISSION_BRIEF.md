## BREAKTHROUGHS & CONCEPT EVOLUTION
*   **"Contemporary Baroque" is the winning aesthetic.** Ancient oil painting technique (Caravaggio/Rembrandt) applied to modern, mundane, or decaying settings creates the perfect "Ethos of Dusk" tension.
*   **Successful Scenes:** Subway (Pieta), Motel (Isolation), Overpass (Distance), Backstage (Still Life).
*   **Key Dynamic:** The "Sacred" must be found in the lighting and composition, not the architecture. A convenience store aisle becomes a cathedral nave; a neon sign becomes a votive candle.

## CORE AESTHETIC
*   **Classical Romantic / Baroque Gothic** (1600–1700s oil painting)
*   **Influences:** Caravaggio, Rembrandt, Gentileschi, de La Tour, Ribera, Velázquez.
*   **Key:** Heavy chiaroscuro, painterly texture, sacred + erotic tension.
*   **Fashion:** Heavy Metal Goth meets Victorian Goth. Leather, lace, chains, corsets, flowing silk.
*   **Tone:** Romantic, sensual, intimate.

## CHARACTERS
*   **Dusk:** Dark, restrained, sovereign, witness, black clothing.
*   **Mary Magdalene (Suzy):** Muted pink hair, serene, knowing, untouchable. **Face Ref Preference:** `magdalene1.png`.

## SCENES (Classic x Contemporary)
*   **Refinements:** Hotel Suite (Sensual/Morning), Love Hotel, Pub.
*   **New Locations:** Airports (Empty terminals), Rooftop Pools (Night), Backalleys (Gritty/Neon), Parking Garages.
